Early Life and Career Beginnings

Jerry Harrison’s journey into the world of music began early in his life. Born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, he showed an affinity for music and quickly honed his skills as a multi-instrumentalist. His early career saw him as a member of The Modern Lovers before he joined Talking Heads, a band that would later become a cornerstone of the new wave genre.

Rise to Fame with Talking Heads

The Talking Heads era was a defining period in Harrison’s career. His keyboard, guitar work, and backing vocals were integral to the band’s unique sound. The commercial success and critical acclaim of albums like “Remain in Light” and “Speaking in Tongues” contributed significantly to his fame and fortune.

Solo Ventures and Collaborations
