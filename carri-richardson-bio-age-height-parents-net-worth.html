In April 2021, Richardson continued to showcase her exceptional talent by clocking a new personal best of 10.72 seconds in the 100 meters, solidifying her position as the sixth-fastest woman of all time at that point, as well as the fourth-fastest American woman in history. However, Richardson’s Olympic journey faced a major setback when it was revealed that she had tested positive for cannabis use following her 100-meter final at the U.S. Trials. After completing a counseling program, Richardson accepted a one-month period of ineligibility that commenced on June 28, 2021.

Enrolling at Louisiana State University in 2018, Richardson continued to make waves in collegiate athletics. At the 2019 NCAA Division I Outdoor Championships, she achieved remarkable feats, setting a collegiate record in the 100 meters with a time of 10.75 seconds and narrowly missing out on victory in the 200 meters by a fraction of a second. Her outstanding performances earned her the reputation of a rising star and led to her decision to forego her remaining collegiate eligibility and sign a professional contract. Richardson now trains under the guidance of former Olympic sprinter Dennis Mitchell and is sponsored by Nike.

While the suspension prevented Richardson from competing in the Olympic Games, she returned to the track at the 2021 Prefontaine Classic, determined to continue her athletic journey. Although her performance resulted in a ninth-place finish, she showcased her resilience and commitment to the sport. In subsequent competitions, Richardson has displayed her exceptional speed and skill, securing victories and making her mark on the international stage.

In April 2023, Richardson delivered another awe-inspiring performance, recording the fourth-fastest 100 meters ever by a woman in all conditions. With a strong tailwind, she blazed through the finish line in an astonishing 10.57 seconds at the Miramar Invitational, further solidifying her status as one of the most promising sprinters of her generation. She followed up this achievement with her first Diamond League victory in May 2023, setting a new meeting record of 10.76 seconds in the 100 meters at the Doha event.
