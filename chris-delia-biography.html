Who is Chris D’Elia?

Chris D’Elia is an American actor, stand-up comedian, writer, and host.

He is well-known for Celeste & Jesse Forever, Glory Daze, Little Evil, Whitney, Undateable, etc. Recently, he made a notable appearance as Henderson in the popular Netflix series, You.

How many siblings does Chris D’Elia have?

Chirs D’Elia was born as Christopher William D’Elia on March 29, 1980, in Montclair, New Jersey.

As of 2022, his age is 42. He was born to parents, Bill D’Elia and Ellie D’Elia(nee Dombroski). His father is a TV producer and director whereas his mother is an interior decorator. He also has a sibling, a younger brother named Matt D’Elia who is also an actor and filmmaker.

He spent his childhood days in Montclair with his parents and brother. His family moved to L.A. when he was about 12. His ethnicity is Italian-American, and Polish.

In which school did Chris D’Elia attend?

For education, he went to La Canada High School. He graduated from there in 1998. Following that, he attended New York University but dropped out from there.

Chris D’Elia: Professional Life, Career

Chris first appeared in a movie after dropping out of college. He started working as a stand-up comedian at the age of 25.

Since then, he has proved himself to be one of the best stand-up comedians in America. He has featured on Comedy Central Presents, Comedy Central’s Live at Gotham, and Showtime’s Live Nude Comedy.

Moreover, he performed in from of a broader audience on the series Glory Daze, which ran on TBS for one season. He played the role of “the Oracle”, William Stankowski. D’Elia gained more attention after he co-starred with comedian Whitney Cummings in the NBC comedy series Whitney for two seasons.

Moreover, he became one of the three hosts of the Ten Minute Podcast alongside Bryan Callen and Will Sasso. He has hosted the weekly podcast titled Congratulations with Chris D’Elia since February 2017. The podcast has charted in the top 20 comedy podcasts in multiple countries from all around the world.

In 2014-2016, NBC sitcom Undateable had the main role as Danny Burton. The show, executive produced by Bill Lawrence is a multi-camera sitcom with a live audience. It often incorporated improv.

In March 2019, it was announced that the comedian would be appearing as Henderson on the second season of the popular Netflix thriller TV series, You.
