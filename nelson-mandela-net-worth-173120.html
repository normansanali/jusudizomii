Nelson Mandela was a South African revolutionary and politician who was born on July 18, 1918 in Mvezo, South Africa. He is best known for his efforts to end the Apartheid state of South Africa and address institutionalized racism and inequality. After spending 27 years in prison, he was released in 1994 and became South Africa's first black chief executive, a position he held for five years.
