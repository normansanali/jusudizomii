WeTV’s Super Size Salon Jamie Lopez has died and fans are mourning her death. They are sending prayers to her online and also paying tribute to her family and friends. Did she leave behind a husband? Who are her parents? How old was she when she died?

All the answers unfold below as you scroll down.

Jamie Lopez Cause Of Death

Jamie Lopez, star of WeTV’s Super Sized Salon is dead. Her company confirmed her death on 19 December. TMZ reported the news of her death first. Source revealed to the outlet that she passed away over the weekend and the cause of her death was a “result of heart complications.”

Shortly after Jamie’s death, her company paid tribute to her with an official statement confirming the loss. “On behalf of the Babydoll Beauty Couture team, we regretfully announce, with great pain, the passing of The Founder & Owner of Babydoll Beauty Couture, The Legendary Jamie Lopez,” the statement that the company shared on social media read. “We ask, on behalf of the Babydoll family, that you allow us time to process this tremendous loss. Further details and arrangements will be announced soon.”

“We have suffered an extraordinary loss and appreciate the time and space to grieve in peace,” the statement continued. “Please keep our Babydoll family & team lifted in your hearts and prayers.”

Jamie Lopez Net Worth

Jamie Lopez, who amassed a net worth of $700 thousand, was the founder and CEO of Babydoll Beauty Couture who founded her business in Las Vegas in 2017 and vowed to make it a safe haven for larger ladies. She told Yahoo Beauty in 2017 of her journey, “I started off as a makeup artist and was highly discriminated against for being a plus-size woman.”

“I was inspired by there being no place I could get my nails or hair done in Vegas that would accommodate my needs as a plus-size woman, and I decided it was time to make some changes for the plus-size women of the world.”

Jamie said her personal experiences factored into her decision to open up a salon. “It makes me very upset to be mistreated because of my size, and not have a place where I can get beautiful,’ she said. ‘When women walk away from getting services done at my salon, I want them to feel beautiful, confident, and sexy.”

Jamie founded her salon in Vegas but had to relocate her business after a hate campaign saw thugs smear feces across its windows and threaten to kill her.

Jamie who was also featured on Barcoft TV’s Shake My Beauty, said: “It’s been a struggle keeping the business open dealing with hate and death threats and other crazy things. People just don’t like fat people, they treat us horribly. They want to make us feel like we’re unwelcome or feel like we don’t belong here.”

She added, “It’s been crazy. My business was vandalized, our salon signs and air conditioning were both broken – and someone literally put poop on our windows. I don’t know if it was dog poop or human poop but it was all over the windows.” The new salon was announced in a grand opening in February while the Club Babydoll night spot goes from strength to strength, she says.

Jamie said: “This is my dream and I am not going to give up on it. I have put my life and everything into this – sacrifice, blood, sweat, tears, everything. Financially it is a huge investment but right now I feel like it’s go big or go home.”
