How did PK Subban start his Professional Career?

The Belleville Bulls of the Ontario Hockey League (OHL) were where Subban began his ice hockey career. After losing to the Kitchener Rangers and the OHL title, he assisted Belleville in reaching the J. Ross Robertson Cup Finals. Two weeks after his final junior game, in May 2009, the NHL’s Montreal Canadiens signed Subban to a three-year, entry-level deal.

He began the 2009–10 American Hockey League season with the Hamilton Bulldogs. On April 26, 2010, while the Canadiens were playing the Washington Capitals in the first round of the Stanley Cup playoffs, Subban was called up from Hamilton. In 2013, he won the Norris Trophy as the league’s top defenceman.

He won two World Junior Championships while competing for Canada’s junior international team. He played for the Canadiens up until the 2015–16 year. In June 2016, he was given to the Nashville Predators in exchange for the defender Shea Weber. He now plays defense for the Predators.
