Leon Wood Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography, How much money make?

In 1984, the Philadelphia 76ers picked Osie Leon Wood III in the first round (tenth overall) of the NBA Draft. Wood was born on March 25, 1962, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and played professionally as a basketball player. Wood, a 6’3″ shooting guard who attended Saint Monica Catholic High School and California State University-Fullerton, played for six different NBA clubs over the course of six seasons. He has played for the Philadelphia 76ers, the Washington Bullets, the New Jersey Nets, the San Antonio Spurs, the Atlanta Hawks, and the Sacramento Kings, among other teams. Additionally, he had a brief period in Germany with MTV 1846 Giessen, with whom he was signed for the playoffs and participated in three games, earning a total of 77 points in the process. During the 1988-1989 season, he also played for CAI Zaragoza (of the ACB League). Wood appeared in 274 games for the NBA and scored a total of 1,742 points during his tenure in the league (6.4 points per game). Bobby Knight coached the 1984 United States Olympic basketball team, which resulted in a gold medal for the United States. Wood works as an NBA official at the moment.
